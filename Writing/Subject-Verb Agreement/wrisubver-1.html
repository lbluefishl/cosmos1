The results of the study suggest that the medication is most effective when taken in 10-gram doses.

Choose 1 Answer:

Explanation

We need to find the subject that the verb refers to. We might be tempted to identify study as the subject, but it is actually the object of a prepositional phrase. $$ \text{The } \underbrace{\text{results}}_\text{subject} \underbrace{\text{of the study}}_\text{prepositional phrase} \underbrace{\text{suggest}}_\text{verb} ... $$ Hiding the prepositional phrase, $$ \text{The results } \text{suggest...} $$ The correct version of each answer choice would be as follows: $$ \text{The results } \colorbox{yellow}{are} \text{ suggesting that the...} $$ $$ \text{The results } \colorbox{yellow}{have} \text{ suggested that the...} $$ $$ \text{The results } \text{suggest}\cancel{\text{s}} \text{ that the...} $$ You may be wondering why the number (singular/plural) of the verb forms differently than a noun. $$ \text{The } \underbrace{\text{results}}_\text{plural} \text{ } \underbrace{\text{suggest}}_\text{plural} \text{ that the...} $$

Thats because in present tense, the plural form of the verb does not have a s added at the end.

Some examples of agreement: $$ \text{Bob chases cats.}$$ $$ \text{Watermelons grow on vines.} $$ $$ \text{Skeletons belong in museums.} $$
