Explanation

Sound is experienced because air molecules bump into each other, creating waves of air. Our ears can detect slight changes in the air, resulting in our perception of sound. These waves vary in shape depending on how they were created:

Higher pitched sounds are due to waves with high frequency.

Amplitute and loudness both refer to the height of each wave (crest to trough).

Timbre is reflected by the shape of the wave, and distinguishes different types of sound production.
