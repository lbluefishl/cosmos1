Given that \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x}-2\) and \(g(x)=\sqrt{x+1} \), find \(g\circ f\).

Approach

The four options reflect the following function compositions:

\( \colorbox{aqua}{$g$} + \colorbox{yellow}{$f$} = \colorbox{aqua}{$\sqrt{x+1}$}+\colorbox{yellow}{$\dfrac{1}{x}-2$} = \sqrt{x+1}+\dfrac{1}{x}-2\\ \) \( \colorbox{aqua}{$g$} \circ \colorbox{yellow}{$f$} = \colorbox{aqua}{$g(\colorbox{yellow}{$f(x)$})$} = \colorbox{aqua}{$\sqrt{\colorbox{yellow}{$\left(\dfrac{1}{x}-2\right)$}+1}$} =\boxed{\sqrt{\dfrac{1}{x}-1}} \\ \) \( \colorbox{yellow}{$f$} \circ \colorbox{aqua}{$g$} = \colorbox{yellow}{$f(\colorbox{aqua}{$g(x)$})$} = \colorbox{yellow}{$\dfrac{1}{\colorbox{aqua}{$\sqrt{x+1}$}}-2$} =\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x+1}}-2 \\ \) \( \colorbox{aqua}{$g$} \cdot \colorbox{yellow}{$f$} = \colorbox{aqua}{$\sqrt{x+1}$} \colorbox{yellow} {$\left( \dfrac{1}{x}-2 \right)$} = \dfrac{\sqrt{x+1}}{x}-2\sqrt{x+1} \)
