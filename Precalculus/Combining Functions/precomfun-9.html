When a certain rock lands on the surface of a lake, it creates a circular ripple that spreads outward at a speed of 20 cm/s. Which pair of functions can be composed to find the area of the circular ripple? In the following functions, \(r\) refers to the radius in centimeters, \(t\) the time in seconds after the rock hits the surface, and \(A\) the area of the ripple.

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach

The radius of the ripple is increasing as a function of time. A simple function to model this would be \( r(t)=20t \).

The area of a circle is a function of its radius. Therefore, \(A(r)=\pi r^2 \)

Sidenote

The composite function would be \(A \circ r \) $$ A \circ r = A(r(t)) $$ $$ = A(20t) $$ $$ = \pi (20t)^2 $$ $$ = 400\pi t^2 $$
