The function \(f\) is defined by the formula \(f(x)=x^3+1\) while the functions \(g\) and \(h\) are defined by the graph and table below. What is the value of \( \left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)(2)-h(2) \)?

$$ \begin{array} {c|c} x & h(x) \\ \hline 1 & 3 \\ \hline 2 & 6 \\ \hline 3 & 9 \\ \hline 4 &12 \\ \hline \end{array} $$

Approach

Evaluate \(f(2), g(2), \text{and } h(2) \). $$ f(x)=x^3+1 $$ $$ f(2)=2^3+1 $$ $$ f(2)=9 $$

$$ g(2)=3 $$ $$ \begin{array} {c|c} x & h(x) \\ \hline 1 & 3 \\ \hline \colorbox{aqua}{2} & \colorbox{aqua}{6} \\ \hline 3 & 9 \\ \hline 4 &12 \\ \hline \end{array} $$ $$h(2)=6 $$ Accordingly, $$ \left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)(2)-h(2) $$ $$ = \frac{f(2)}{g(2)}-h(2) $$ $$ =\frac{9}{3}-6 $$ $$ = 3-6 $$ $$ = \boxed{-3} $$
