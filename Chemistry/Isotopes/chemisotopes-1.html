Uranium-235 and uranium-238 differ in the number of which of the following?

Choose 1 Answer:

Explanation

Uranium-235 refers to uranium with a mass number of 235. Similarly, uranium-238 would have a mass number of 238. Protons and neutrons each contribute 1 to the mass number, whereas electrons are negligible in mass and contribute 0. All uranium atoms have the same number of protons (92). Therefore, we can calculate the mass contribution from the neutrons by the following: $$ \ce {^235U} $$ \[\text{mass number} = \text{neutrons} + \text{protons} \] \[235=\text{neutrons}+92 \] \[\text{neutrons}=143\] \( \ce{^238U}\) has a mass number 3 greater, so it should have \(143+3=146\) neutrons.
