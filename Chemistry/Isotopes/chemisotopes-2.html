A certain atom has 19 protons and 21 neutrons. Which of the following symbols corresponds to this atom?

Choose 1 Answer:

Explanation

Nuclear notation can be written as the following: $$ \ce{^{A}_{Z}X } $$

A=Mass number

Z=Protons

X=Element symbol

Note that it can also be written like the following, since the symbol and proton number are redundant.

$$ \ce{^{A}_{}X } $$ An atom with 19 protons will always be Potassium (K). Its mass number would also be the number of protons + neutrons (19+21=40).

Accordingly, if A=40, Z=19, and X=K, $$ \boxed{\ce{^{40}_{19}K} } $$
