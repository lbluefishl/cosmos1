A company is forming a committee to discuss future plans. Of those invited to join the committee, 10% are managers, 35% are standard employees, 20% are shareholders, and the remaining 7 individuals are customers. How many more shareholders were invited to join the committee than managers?

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach

The customers must make up the remaining percentage of the total.

$$ \text{Customer percentage}=100-(10+35+20) $$ $$ = 100-65 $$ $$ = 35\% $$

We can find the total number of individuals at the meeting using the values for the customers:

$$ 0.35(\text{ Total})= 7 $$ $$ \text{ Total} = 20 $$

Shareholders make up 20% of the committee whereas managers make up 10%. Therefore,

$$ \text{Difference}=(0.20-0.10)(20) $$ $$ = (0.10)(20) $$ $$ = \boxed{2} $$
