What is the value of \(x-y\), given the following system of equations?

$$ 7x+3y=8$$ $$ 6x-3y=5$$

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach 1

Because the equations are arranged so that corresponding terms are matching, we can use elimination and add each corresponding term. $$ 7x+3y=8$$ $$ 6x-3y=5$$ $$ \begin{array}{c c c} &7x & 3y & 8 \\ +&6x & -3y & 5 \\ \hline \end{array} $$ $$13x=13$$ $$x=1$$ Substituting \(x\) into either equation, $$ 7x+3y=8$$ $$7(1)+3y=8$$ $$7+3y=8$$ $$3y=1$$ $$y=\frac{1}{3}$$ Finally, $$x-y = 1-\frac{1}{3}$$ $$ = \boxed{\frac{2}{3}}$$

Approach 2

Although elimination is efficient, we can also use substitution to eliminate a variable. Note that the \(3y\) term is present in both equations. $$ 7x+\colorbox{yellow}{\(3y\)}=8$$ $$ 6x-\colorbox{yellow}{\(3y\)}=5$$ Solving for \(3y\) in the first equation, $$7x+3y=8$$ $$3y=8-7x$$ Substituting this value into the second equation, $$ 6x-3y=5$$ $$6x-(8-7x)=5$$ $$6x-8+7x=5$$ $$13x=13$$ $$x=1$$ Substituting \(x\) into either equation, $$ 7x+3y=8$$ $$7(1)+3y=8$$ $$7+3y=8$$ $$3y=1$$ Finally, $$x-y = 1-\frac{1}{3}$$ $$ = \boxed{\frac{2}{3}}$$
