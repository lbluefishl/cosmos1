Given the following equation, what is the value of \(2x+1\)? $$6x+3=16$$

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach 1

Solve for \(x\) $$6x+3=16$$ $$6x=13$$ $$x=\frac{13}{6}$$ Substitute the value of \(x\) into \(2x+1\). $$2 \left(\frac{13}{6}\right)+1$$ $$\frac{13}{3}+1$$ $$\frac{13}{3}+\frac{3}{3}$$ $$\frac{16}{3}$$

Approach 2

Recognize that \(6x+3\) is simply \(2x+1\) tripled. You could also factor to come up with a similar conclusion. $$6x+3=16$$ $$3(2x+1)=16$$ We can simply divide by \(3\) on both sides to isolate \(2x+1\). $$2x+1=\frac{16}{3}$$
