What value of \(t\) is the solution to the equation below? $$ \frac{3}{4}t=\frac{5}{3} $$

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach 1

Rewrite the fraction and cross multiply. $$ \frac{3}{4}t=\frac{5}{3} $$ $$ \frac{3t}{4}\xcancel{=}\frac{5}{3} $$ $$ 3t(3)=4(5) $$ $$ 9t=20 $$ $$ \boxed{t=\frac{20}{9}} $$

Approach 2

Multiply both sides by the reciprocal of the \(t\) coefficient. (Same as dividing each side by the coefficient) $$ \frac{3}{4}t=\frac{5}{3} $$ $$ \left(\frac{4}{3}\right)\frac{3}{4}t=\frac{5}{3}\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) $$ $$ \left( \frac{\cancel{4}}{\bcancel{3}}\right)\frac{\bcancel{3}}{\cancel{4}}t=\frac{5}{3}\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) $$ $$ t=\frac{5\cdot4}{3\cdot3}$$ $$ \boxed{t=\frac{20}{9}} $$
