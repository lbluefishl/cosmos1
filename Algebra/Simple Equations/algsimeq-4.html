If \(6 \cdot 2k = 72\), what is the value of \(2k-4\)?

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach 1

Solve for \(k\). $$6 \cdot 2k = 72 $$ $$ 2k=12 $$ $$ k=6 $$ Substitute our value of \(k\) into the expression \(2k-4\). $$ 2k-4$$ $$2(6)-4$$ $$12-4$$ $$8$$

Approach 2

We can reduce the number of steps by recognizing that \(2k\) is present in the initial equation and final expression. $$6 \cdot 2k = 72 $$ $$ 2k=12 $$ Subtract 4 from both sides. $$2k-4=12-4$$ $$2k-4=8$$
