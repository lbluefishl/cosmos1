What is the simplified form of the following? $$ 2x^2 \cdot 3x^3 $$

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach 1

One way to visualize what is going on is to write out each individual term. $$ 2x^2 \cdot 3x^3 $$ $$ 2 \cdot x \cdot x \cdot 3 \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x $$ $$ \underbrace{2 \cdot 3}_\text{constants}\cdot \underbrace{ x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x}_\text{variables} \tag*{rearrange constants and variables}$$ $$ = \boxed{6x^5} $$

Approach 2

Given exponential terms with the same base, we can multiply by adding the exponents: $$x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b} $$ Accordingly, $$2x^2 \cdot 3x^3$$ $$2 \cdot 3 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^3$$ $$=6x^{2+3}$$ $$=\boxed{6x^5}$$
