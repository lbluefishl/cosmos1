The equation below shows the relationship between positive numbers \(a\), \(b\), \(x\), \(y\), and \(c\). Which of the following correctly expresses \(a\) in terms of \(b\), \(x\), \(y\), and \(c\)? $$ax+by=c$$

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach

If you have difficulties with these types of problem, try replacing variables with numbers and anticipating the steps you would normally use.

With variables $$ax+by=c$$ $$ax=c-by$$ $$\boxed{x=\frac{c-by}{x}}$$ With numbers $$a(1)+(2)(3)=(4)$$ $$a(1)=(4)-(2)(3)$$ $$a=\frac{(4)-(2)(3)}{(1)}$$

Sidenote

Did this question seem familiar? You've probably done a similar rearrangement many times before, when changing a line in standard form to slope-intercept form: $$ 5x+3y=6$$ $$ 3y=-5x+6$$ $$y=-\frac{5}{3}x+2$$
