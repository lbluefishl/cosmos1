The mass of the Earth is approximately \(6 \times 10^{24}\) kg. The Moon, with center approximately \(3.8 \times 10^8\) m from the Earth's center, has a mass of approximately \(7.3 \times 10^{22}\) kg. Which of the following is the best approximation of the gravitational force of the Earth on the Moon?

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach

The gravitational force between two objects with masses \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) and of \(r\) distance apart takes the form:

$$ F=G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2} $$ The question gives us the approximate values:

\(r = 3.8 \times 10^8 \) m

\(m_1= 6 \times 10^{24} \) kg

\(m_2= 7.3\times 10^{22} \) kg

Gravitational Constant:

\(G= 6.674\times 10^{-11} \) \(\text{m}^3\cdot \text{kg}^{-1}\cdot \text{s}^{-2} \)

$$ F = (6.674\times10^{-11})\frac{( 6 \times 10^{24})( 7.3\times 10^{22})}{(3.8 \times 10^8)^2} $$ $$ \approx 2.02 \times 10^{20} \text{ N} $$ The closest answer choice is \( \boxed{ 10^{20} \text{ N}} \)
