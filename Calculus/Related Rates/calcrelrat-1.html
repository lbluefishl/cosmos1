What is the radius of an expanding circle when the area in square units of the circle is increasing four times as fast as its radius in linear units?

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach

The area of a circle can written as: $$ A=\pi r^2$$ If we take the derivative of both sides with respect to time, $$ \frac{dA}{dt}=\pi \frac{d}{dt}(r^2)$$ $$ \frac{dA}{dt}=\pi \cdot 2r\cdot \frac{dr}{dt}$$ $$ \frac{dA}{dt}=2\pi r\frac{dr}{dt}$$ The question mentions that the rate that the area is increasing is 4 times that of the radius: $$ \frac{dA}{dt}=4\frac{dr}{dt} $$ Substituting this into the equation we found earlier, $$ 4\frac{dr}{dt}=2\pi r\frac{dr}{dt}$$ $$ \boxed{\frac{2}{\pi}}=r$$
