Approach

Break apart the fraction in order to use the power rule and rule for natural logarithms. $$ \int \limits_1^e \left( \frac{x^2-1}{x} \right)   dx = \int \limits_1^e \left( \frac{x^2}{x}-\frac{1}{x} \right)   dx $$ $$ = \int \limits_1^e \left( x-\frac{1}{x} \right)   dx $$ $$ = \left[ \frac{1}{2}x^2-\ln |x| \right]_1^e$$ $$ = \frac{1}{2}(e)^2-\ln|e|-\left(\frac{1}{2}(1)^2-\ln|1|\right) $$ $$ = \frac{1}{2}e^2-1-\left(\frac{1}{2}-0\right)$$ $$ = \frac{1}{2}e^2-1-\frac{1}{2}$$ $$ =\boxed{\frac{e^2}{2}-\frac{3}{2}} $$
