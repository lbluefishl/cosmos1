\( \int \limits_0^{15} \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{1+x}} =\)

Choose 1 Answer:

Approach

Use the substitution rule. $$ u=1+x$$ $$ \frac{du}{dx}=1$$

Upper bound
$$ x=15,  u=1+15 = 16$$
Lower bound
$$ x=0,   u=1+0 = 1 $$
$$ \int \limits_0^{15} \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{1+x}} = \int \limits_1^{16} \dfrac{du}{\sqrt{u}} $$ We can rewrite the expression to make it easier to integrate: $$ \int \limits_1^{16} \dfrac{du}{\sqrt{u}} = \int \limits_1^{16} u^{-\frac{1}{2}} du $$ $$= \left[ 2u^{\frac{1}{2}} \right]_1^{16} = \left[ 2\sqrt{u} \right]_1^{16} $$ $$ =2(\sqrt{16}-\sqrt{1}) $$ $$ =2(4-1)$$ $$ =\boxed{6}$$
