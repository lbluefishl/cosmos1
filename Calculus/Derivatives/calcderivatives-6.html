Approach

To calculate the first derivative, use the product and chain rule. $$ f(x)=(x-1)^2\sin{x} $$ $$ f'(x)=(x-1)^2\cos{x}+\sin{x}\cdot2(x-1)\cdot1 $$ $$ = (x-1)^2\cos{x}+2(x-1)\sin{x} $$ Substitute \(x=0\) $$ f'(0)=(0-1)^2\cos{0}+2(0-1)\sin{0}$$ $$= 1+0$$ $$=\boxed{1}$$
