Approach

Rewrite \(f(x)\) into fractional exponent form. $$ f(x)=\ln(\sqrt{x})=\ln{x^{\frac{1}{2}}} $$ $$ f(x)= \frac{1}{2}\ln{x} \tag*{log of power rule}$$ Calculating the first derivative, $$ f'(x)= \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{1}{x} $$ $$ f'(x)= \frac{1}{2x} $$ Again, rewrite into fractional exponent form to calculate the second derivative, $$ f''(x)=\frac{1}{2}x^{-1} $$ $$ =-1 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot x^{-2} $$ $$ = \boxed{-\frac{1}{2x^2}}$$
