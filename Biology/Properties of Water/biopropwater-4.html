Water striders are insects that can walk across the surface of calm water. What is the best explanation of this phenomenon?

Explanation

Water molecules at the surface cling to each other due to the hydrogen bonding. At the surface, The force of attraction is even greater since there are relatively fewer surrounding water molecules. Though insects are light, the phenomenon is primarily caused by surface tension, as described earlier. The force of gravity on the insect is not strong enough to break the surface tension.
