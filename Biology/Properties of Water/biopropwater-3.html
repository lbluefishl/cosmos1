Despite hot summer days, the water in bodies of water typically do not heat up or evaporate significantly. What properties are responsible for these observations?

Explanation

Similar to water's heat of fusion, the heat of vaporization of water is quite large. This translates to small changes in temperature during summer. You have likely experienced this as well, such as when dipping your feet into the ocean under a blazing sun.

Cohesion refers to the tendency for water molecules to be attracted to each other. For water to evaporate, the molecules must escape the intermolecular forces that hold them together.
