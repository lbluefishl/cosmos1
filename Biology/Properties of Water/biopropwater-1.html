Which of the following is responsible for the properties of water: high heat capacity, surface tension, cohension, and adhesion?

Choose 1 Answer:

Explanation

Hydrogen bonding, though a weak force on its own, is strong when there are a lot of water molecules. The cumulative attractive force results in the properties of water.

You can think of surface tension, cohesion, and adhesion as stickiness; water molecules like to cling to each other and other hydrophilic objects due to the attractive force of hydrogen bonding.

High heat capacity means it takes a lot of energy to raise the average speed of molecules. In other words, it takes a lot of energy to break hydrogen bonds and get the molecules to fly around faster (such as when boiling water).
