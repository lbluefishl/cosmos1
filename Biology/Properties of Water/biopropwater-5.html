One characteristic property of water is adhesion, the ability for water molecules to stick to other substances. Which of the following observations is least caused by adhesion?

Explanation

Ice floating on water is due to differences in density. Ice and water are also the same molecule, whereas adhesion typically refers to interaction between two different substances.

Capillary action results from a combination of adhesion and cohesion. Water molecules stick together and along with the sides of the inner plant as they travel upwards.

Water molecules form hydration shells around polar molecules, thus dissolving them. Similar to hydrogen bonding, the negative region of water (oxygen) is attracted to positive portions of molecules.

A meniscus is the macro representation of water molecules sticking to the sides of the graduated cylinder.
