During colder months, ponds and lakes often freeze. Despite this, fish and other organisms often survive till thaw. Which property of water contributes most to this observance?

Explanation

For most molecules, density increases as the molecules transition from liquid to solid. However, water's density is greatest around 4°C. This is because the crystalline structure of ice result in a large lattice structure. This structure forms due to the shape of water molecules and hydrogen bonding.

This property results in floating ice instead of ice sinking to the bottom of the lake. The surface ice then forms an insulating layer to protect the liquid water below. In addition, water's high heat of fusion makes it energetically costly for liquid water to freeze.
