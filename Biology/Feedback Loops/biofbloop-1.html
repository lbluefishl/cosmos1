Explanation

The first two options may be true in the sense that thyroxine remains, but the last part of both statements is incorrect. Thyroxine inhibits the Anterior Pituitary, which results in less TSH production. Therefore, a reduction in Thyroxine will result in reduced inhibition of the Anterior Pituitary, resulting in normal or enhanced TSH production. The TSH normally would be used in the Thyroid Gland, but due to its removal TSH would instead accumulate in the bloodstream.
